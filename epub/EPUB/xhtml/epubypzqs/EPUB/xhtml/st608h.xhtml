Present in a variety of ways (e.g. confusion, drowsiness, headache, vomiting, hiccups, seizures).

Investigate with a contrast-enhanced CT or an MRI in patients where treatment would be considered.

If symptoms are problematic, give dexamethasone 8 or 16mg daily. Give as a single dose in the morning or with divided doses in the morning and at midday. In patients unable to swallow, it can be given by subcutaneous injection.

For solitary lesions, neurosurgery may be an option.

If there are multiple lesions or if neurosurgery is not appropriate for a single lesion, treatment with radiotherapy can sometimes improve symptoms and prolong life by many months, or even longer.

Wean dexamethasone post-radiotherapy over 4 weeks unless symptoms flare-up.

Adverse effects of cerebral irradiation include memory loss, change in personality and hair loss.
