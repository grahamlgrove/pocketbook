Present in a variety of ways (e.g. confusion, drowsiness, headache, vomiting, hiccups, seizures). If symptoms are problematic, give dexamethasone 8 or 16mg daily (plus a proton pump inhibitor). Arrange contrast-enhanced CT or an MRI in patients where treatment would be considered.

For solitary lesions, neurosurgery may be an option. If there are multiple lesions or if neurosurgery is not appropriate for a single lesion, treatment with radiotherapy can sometimes improve symptoms and prolong life by a number of months. Wean dexamethasone post-radiotherapy over 4 weeks unless symptoms flare-up. Adverse effects of cerebral irradiation include memory loss, change in personality and hair loss.
