As patients near the end of their life and are too drowsy to swallow it is often helpful to give background analgesia and benzodiazepines via a continuous subcutaneous infusion.

  • Morphine 10mg via CSCI over 24 hours
  • Clonazepam 1mg via CSCI over 24 hours

Don't forget to write up appropriate PRN medications, e.g.

  • Morphine 2.5mg 1-hourly subcut PRN for pain
  • Clonazepam 0.5mg 1-hourly subcut PRN for agitation

 

Doses of opioid and benzodiazepines may need to be higher in patients who have been on background doses of these longer term.
