For acute airway obstruction, such as due to rapid tumour growth -->

If life-saving acute treatment is appropriate
Get immediate help to maintain the airway from a paramedic, anaesthetist or ENT specialist
For malignant causes, give high dose steroids (dexamethasone 16mg subcut or IM) and get urgent advice from an oncologist

 

If terminal care is appropriate
Treat with high doses of opioids and benzodiazepines (similar to the treatment for massive, terminal haemorrhages)
