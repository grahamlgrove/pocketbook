In palliative care patients, pain is common, but by no means universal.

Pain can usually be well managed through a combination of specific targeted interventional techniques (e.g. radiotherapy or nerve blocks), appropriate analgesia (often opioids are required) and appropriate psychological support.

This chapter offers advice on the following specific scenarios:

  • Incident pain
  • Neuropathic pain
  • Painful bony metastases
  • Muscular spasm in neurological conditions
  • Colicky abdominal pain
  • Rectal pain
  • Refractory pain
