Rectal pain

Refer early as this is often difficult to treat. Options include:

First-line:

  • Opioids
  • Steroids or NSAIDs (e.g. diclofenac 100mg suppository)

If pain persists, consider:

  • Neuropathic agents (e.g. pregabalin (PBS streamlined authority))
  • GTN (try sublingually initially, or topically if sphincter spasm suspected) (also helps oesophageal pain)
  • Topical anaesthetics
  • Specialist referral (options include nifedipine, methadone or lidocaine)
