• Allow an awake patient to take sips of fluids and small mouthfuls of food if the patient requests it
  • Keeping the mouth moist is believed to minimise thirst
  • Parenteral fluids in most cases are ineffective for thirst and may worsen dyspnoea. Supporting families in this decision is important.
  • Taper any supplemental nutrition (e.g. via a PEG) as this is usually inappropriate and worsens aspiration risk.
