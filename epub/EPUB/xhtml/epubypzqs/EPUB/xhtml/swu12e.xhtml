• Long-term medications should usually be ceased unless they reduce symptoms
  • Epilepsy
    • Some epilepsy medications can be given by subcutaneous infusion (e.g. levetiracetam)
    • To reduce seizure risk in patients with epilepsy, give clonazepam 2mg over 24 hours via CSCI
    • See Section on Seizures
  • Diabetes mellitus
    • For patients with type I diabetes, to reduce symptoms of diabetic ketoacidosis, give half the previous total daily dose of insulin as a single daily dose of a long-acting insulin
    • Cease for type II diabetes mellitus
  • Steroids
    • Steroids should only be continued if used for symptom control, otherwise rapidly wean or cease them
    • Dexamethasone can be given subcutaneously
  • Parkinson's disease
    • Continue anti-Parkinson medication until oral route lost
    • Start a rotigotine patch once the oral route is lost
    • In the imminently dying, use clonazepam 2mg over 24 hours via CSCI (or midazolam (non-PBS) 10 mg over 24 hours via CSCI) for rigidity.
