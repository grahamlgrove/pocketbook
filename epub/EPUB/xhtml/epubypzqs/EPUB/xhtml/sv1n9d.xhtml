When suspected, CT-PA is the investigation of choice. If contraindicated (e.g. raised creatinine or contrast allergy) then a VQ scan +/- US leg of veins is an alternative.

First-line treatment is long-term Factor XA inhibitors, e.g. enoxaparin 1.5mg/kg subcut daily. Dose reduction required in renal failure or for thrombocytopaenia.

If very high bleeding risk, IVC filter insertion (speak to interventional radiologist) may be considered instead of anti-coagulation
