If pain is poorly controlled but opioids are having some benefit, the long-acting background opioid dose can be gradually increased taking the previous 24-hour breakthrough requirements into account.

In general, it is safest to increase the background dose of opioid by no more than 33% in one go.

For example, if a patient on 100mg of daily long-acting oral morphine has 3 breakthroughs of 10mg oral morphine in 24-hours, the oral long-acting dose could be increased to 130mg daily.

If increasing doses of opioids are not helping, think about other analgesic options.
