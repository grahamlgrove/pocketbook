For patients gradually declining with advanced, metastatic cancer the Palliative Prognostic Index (PPI) can be a useful guide.

  • If the PPI > 6 -> death is likely within a month

Specific complications and scenarios

  • Obstructive Uropathy: If a patient develops acute renal failure with anuria due to severe obstructive uropathy and this is not relieved via stenting -> creatinine will typically rise 50-100 daily with death likely within weeks, especially once creatinine passes 1,000
  • Biliary obstruction: If a patient develops severe bile duct obstruction due to tumour and this is not relieved via stenting -> bilirubin will typically rise 15-30 daily with death is likely within weeks, especially once bilirubin passes 400
  • Acute myeloid leukaemia: If a patient has a rapidly rising white cell count and a short doubling time of a few days in the context of untreated acute myeloid leukaemia -> death likely within weeks, especially once the total white cell count passes 400
