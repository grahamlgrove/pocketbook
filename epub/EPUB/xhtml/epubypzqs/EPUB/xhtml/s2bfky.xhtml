For opioid naïve patients, dose is age dependent.

  • For the elderly – half the typical dose

For young adults – increase the typical dose by 50%

  • In renal failure, consider avoiding morphine
  • Hydromorphone is better in renal failure (or fentanyl for severe renal failure)
Initial doses for opioid-naïve patients
Morphine 2.5mg hourly subcut PRN - 1st line
If renal impairment:

Hydromorphone 0.5mg hourly subcut PRN

OR

Fentanyl (non-PBS) 50micrograms hourly subcut PRN
In opioid tolerant patients

The above doses will be inadequate

In these patients, change oral background opioids to CSCI pump and ensure appropriate subcut PRN doses (see Equi-analgesic opioid doses page)
