Palliative care is the care of patients who have an advanced, progressive illness that is not curable, and in general, will probably result in death within weeks or months (as opposed to many years). Given this context, there is an emphasis on quality of life and symptom control. At times there will also be a focus on active interventions to reverse acute complications of illness and, in doing so, prolong a person’s life. At other times there will be a focus on maintaining comfort and so avoiding interventions and instead allowing a natural death.

Palliative care extends past the physical to embrace emotional, spiritual and social aspects of the care of patients and their families. It is as much an art as it is a science. The emphasis of this book, however, is about succinct principles and medication guidelines, with the assumption that other aspects of the biopsychosocial determinants of health are also being explored and managed.
