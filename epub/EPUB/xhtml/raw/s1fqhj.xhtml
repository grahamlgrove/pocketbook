Question to answer:

  • Is ascites present?
  • Is a drainable amount of ascites present?

Example clinical scenario: A patient has an uncomfortable, distended abdomen. Could the distension and discomfort be due to ascites and is there enough to make drainage both straightforward and potentially helpful in relieving symptoms?

Ultrasound protocol:

  • Probe to use: 1-5 MHz
  • Patient position: Lying supine with abdomen region exposed
  • Move the probe over the right inguinal and lumbar and umbilical quadrants noting the presence or absence of ascites
  • Move the probe over the left inguinal and lumbar and umbilical quadrants noting the presence or absence of ascites
  • If ascites is present determine visually the pocket that appears largest and deepest
  • Measure the depth of the ascites
