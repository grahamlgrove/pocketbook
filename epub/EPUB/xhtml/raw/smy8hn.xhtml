Hyperhidrosis (excessive sweating) can be a problem because:

  • It is an unpleasant symptom
  • It can reduce adhesion of transdermal patches
  • It can cause fluid loss

Consider underlying causes including:

  • Infection with fever
  • A paraneoplastic phenomenon (e.g. carcinoid tumours, lymphomas)
  • Iatrogenic causes such as with hormonal antineoplastic drugs in breast and prostate cancer
  • Associated with emotional distress or physical symptoms (such as pain)

Non-pharmacological measures include cooling the skin (e.g. with a fan and loose cotton clothing) and oral fluids for rehydration

Trial of an anti-pyretic such as

  • Paracetamol 1g four times a day
  • NSAID, e.g. naproxen 500mg twice daily

Sweating associated with haematological malignancies frequently occurs at night and may improve with low-dose steroids

Hot flushes in patients with breast or prostate cancer may improve with venlafaxine, gabapentin or pregabalin (non-PBS indication)
